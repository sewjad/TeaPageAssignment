Tea Industry

Plucked Tea Leaves

Sri Lanka is the fourth highest Tea producer in the World. The tea industry introduced by the British plays an important role in the economy of Sri Lanka. For more than a century, tea has been the highest provider of employment, export incomes and government revenue. By the independence of Sri Lanka in 1948, tea along with rubber and coconut aided more than 92 % of total export incomes of the country.

Sri Lanka is privileged with the cooler, mistier climate and suitable soil and slops which aids the tea industry.The resources of tea industry were well applied in Sri Lanka with the support of private enterprises and state of plantations. The tea sector still continues to occupy an important place in the economy of Sri Lanka.

History of the Tea Industry in Sri Lanka

History of tea

Tea was introduced to Sri Lanka just after coffee was ruined by Coffee Leaf Rust in the 19th century..Sri Lanka received the first tea plant from Assam in the 1939 when the British were governing Sri Lanka, where they brought more economical, social, political and cultural alterations to Sri Lanka. Not until 1867 tea could be cultivated commercially and it is James Taylor, A Scottish planter who gained basic knowledge from North India and proved on Loolkandura plantations.

The success story of tea planting attracted English planters and more estates were established, replacing existed coffee fields. Consequently, as the coffee area was not big enough to meet the demand for tea cultivation, the area extended further into the hill country, finally into the Uva and the country soon became a prominent tea producer.
